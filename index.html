What is reflection and why use Gibbs?

Reflection is a structured way to learn from experience. The Gibbs Reflective Cycle provides a practical, step-by-step framework to review an event, understand it, and decide how to improve next time.

  • Turn experience into actionable learning
  • Strengthen academic writing and self-awareness
  • Plan concrete, realistic next steps

The six stages at a glance

  1. Description — What happened?
  2. Feelings — What were you thinking and feeling?
  3. Evaluation — What went well, and what didn’t?
  4. Analysis — Why did it happen? What does theory suggest?
  5. Conclusion — What have you learned? What else could you have done?
  6. Action Plan — What will you do next time, specifically?

Tip: Use concise, factual Description; be honest but brief with Feelings; be balanced in Evaluation; connect ideas in Analysis; be specific in Conclusion and Action Plan.
